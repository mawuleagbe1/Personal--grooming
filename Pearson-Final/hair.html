What's Your Hair Type?

Understanding your hair is the first step to proper care

Straight Hair

  • Naturally smooth texture
  • Prone to oiliness
  • Can lack volume

Wavy Hair

  • S-shaped pattern
  • Prone to frizz
  • Combination texture

Curly Hair

  • Defined spiral pattern
  • Needs extra moisture
  • Prone to dryness

Coily/Kinky Hair

  • Tight zig-zag pattern
  • Fragile structure
  • Needs intense moisture

Understanding Hair Porosity

Why it matters and how to care for your porosity level

Quick Porosity Test:

Place a strand of clean hair in a glass of water. Does it:

‚Üë

Float on top

Low porosity: Cuticles are tight, products sit on hair

‚Üí

Float in middle

Medium porosity: Cuticles are slightly raised, ideal balance

‚Üì

Sink quickly

High porosity: Cuticles are open, absorbs quickly but loses moisture

Care Tips:

  • Use heat to help products penetrate (warm towel wrap)
  • Look for lightweight, liquid-based products
  • Clarify regularly to prevent buildup

The Essential Hair Care Routine

1

Cleansing

Frequency: 1-2 times weekly (varies by hair type)

Key Tips: Focus on scalp, use sulfate-free for curly/coily hair

2

Conditioning

Frequency: Every wash day

Key Tips: Apply mid-length to ends, use wide-tooth comb to distribute

Professional Styling Techniques

Master these looks at home with our step-by-step guides

Beginner

The Perfect Blowout

To achieve the perfect blowout, you'll need a round brush for smoothness and volume, a heat protectant to shield your hair from damage, and a blow dryer to style and set your look.

Intermediate

Protective Braiding

To achieve protective braiding, start by using a rat-tail comb to section your hair neatly, ensuring clean parts for styles like cornrows or box braids while gently detangling to prevent breakage. Next, apply edge control to smooth down flyaways and baby hairs, using a brush or your fingertips to lay edges neatly before setting them with a silk scarf for extra hold.
