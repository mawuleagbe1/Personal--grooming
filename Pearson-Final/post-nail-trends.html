The Color Palette of Summer 2025

These are the shades dominating runways and salons this season

Citrus Zest

A vibrant orange-yellow hybrid that evokes fresh-squeezed juices. Pantone's Color of the Season works beautifully on both short and long nails.

Aqua Spritz

This translucent aquamarine captures the essence of tropical waters. The barely-there finish makes it perfect for the "wet look" trend.

Solar Flare

A neon coral with pink undertones that glows under sunlight. Surprisingly versatile, it complements all skin tones from fair to deep.

Lavender Mist

The cooler counterpart to last year's lilac craze. This airy pastel provides a soft contrast to the season's brighter hues.

Top Design Innovations

Creative techniques redefining summer manicures

Architectural Negative Space

The minimalist trend evolves with geometric cut-outs and asymmetrical patterns. Nail artists are using precise vinyl stencils to create sharp lines and unexpected shapes that expose the natural nail beneath.

"Start with a nude base and use fine striping tape to map your negative spaces before polishing" - Mei Kawajiri, Creative Director at Nail Labo Tokyo

Watercolor Washes

Soft, blended color gradients mimic the effect of watercolor paintings. The technique involves layering sheer polishes with a damp brush for a dreamy, translucent finish. Popular combinations include peach-to-yellow and mint-to-lavender transitions.

"Apply a white base first to make pastel colors pop, and work quickly before each layer dries" - Sofia Sanchez, Celebrity Manicurist

Molten Metal Foils

Press-on metallic foils create a liquid metal effect that catches sunlight beautifully. The trend features irregular applications that look like melted gold, silver, or copper dripping down the nail. Works particularly well with deep berry or emerald bases.

"Use a top coat with slight texture to help foil adhere better, and always seal with a glossy top coat" - Leighton Denny, British Nail Expert

Nail Shapes Dominating 2025

From ultra-short to dramatic silhouettes

Soft Ballerina

A gentler take on the classic almond shape with slightly squared-off tips. The tapered sides elongate fingers while the flat tip provides stability. Ideal length is 3-5mm beyond the fingertip.

Petite Square

The "office-friendly" length makes a comeback with perfectly straight edges and corners softened just enough to prevent snagging. Typically 1-2mm past the fingertip, this shape works beautifully with French manicures.

Popularity
Durability

The Edge

For the bold, this architectural shape features sharp 45-degree angles at the tips. The dramatic silhouette requires acrylic or hard gel extensions and looks best with minimalist color schemes to highlight the form.

Technique Spotlight

Innovative application methods gaining popularity

Water Marbling 2.0

The updated version of this classic technique uses silicone mats instead of water. Drops of polish are swirled directly on the mat, then transferred to nails using a stamper. Creates more consistent results with less polish waste.

Key Materials:

  • Silicone nail art mat
  • Stamper with jelly head
  • Quick-dry polishes
  • Orange wood stick for swirling

Celebrity Nail Looks

Trendsetting manicures spotted on the stars

Zendaya's Solar Gradient

The actress debuted this sunset-inspired look at a film premiere, featuring a seamless transition from yellow at the cuticle to orange at the tips. Nail artist Kim Truong used an airbrush for the smooth fade effect.

Key Elements:

  • Ballerina shape extensions
  • Matte top coat
  • Thin gold foil accents

Harry's Chrome Tips

The musician's signature gender-neutral style features short, square natural nails with mirror-like chrome on just the tips. Achieved with a black base coat and chrome powder buffed onto the free edge only.

Key Elements:

  • Natural nail length
  • High-shine chrome powder
  • Deep sidewall cleaning

UV Protection

Apply SPF to hands and nails before sun exposure. Gel polish can act like a magnifying glass, increasing risk of sunspots on nails. Reapply sunscreen after swimming or every 2 hours.

Water Resistance

Chlorine and saltwater weaken nail bonds. Apply a thick top coat before swimming and moisturize cuticles afterward with oils containing jojoba or vitamin E.
