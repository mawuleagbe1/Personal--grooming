Find Your Skin Type

Customize your routine based on your skin's unique needs

Oily Skin

Oily skin is characterized by a shiny complexion, enlarged pores, and a tendency to develop acne. This skin type often produces excess sebum, which can lead to clogged pores and breakouts if not managed with the right skincare routine.

Dry Skin

Dry skin often feels tight, rough, or flaky, and may show visible fine lines. It can be prone to irritation and may appear dull or lackluster, especially after cleansing or exposure to dry environments.

Combination

Combination skin features an oily T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin), dry or normal cheeks, and variable pore sizes. This skin type requires a balanced approach, targeting oil control in some areas while providing hydration in others.

Sensitive

Sensitive skin is easily irritated and prone to redness, often reacting to various skincare products or environmental factors. It requires gentle, fragrance-free formulations to minimize discomfort and maintain a healthy skin barrier.

The 2-Step Essential Routine

Daily practices for all skin types

1

Cleanse

Remove dirt, oil, and impurities without stripping skin

Recommended Products:

  • CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser
  • La Roche-Posay Toleriane
  • Vanicream Gentle Cleanser

How to use the cleanser:

To properly cleanse your skin, start by choosing a cleanser suited to your skin type—gentle, creamy formulas for dry or sensitive skin, foaming or gel-based options for oily or acne-prone skin, and balanced cleansers for combination or normal skin. Before applying the cleanser, wash your hands to avoid transferring dirt and bacteria, then wet your face with lukewarm water to avoid stripping natural oils. Take a small amount of cleanser, lather it between your hands, and massage it onto your face using gentle circular motions, focusing on areas prone to oiliness or congestion. After cleansing for about 20–30 seconds, rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water to remove all residue, then pat your skin dry with a clean towel—avoid rubbing to prevent irritation..

2

Tone

Balance pH levels and prepare skin for treatment

Recommended Products:

  • Thayers Witch Hazel Toner
  • Paula's Choice Enriched Calming Toner
  • Fresh Rose Deep Hydration Toner

How to use this Product:

After cleansing, apply your chosen toner—Thayers Witch Hazel Toner, Paula’s Choice Enriched Calming Toner, or Fresh Rose Deep Hydration Toner—by soaking a cotton pad or pressing a few drops into your hands, then gently swipe or pat it over your face and neck. For Thayers (best for oily or acne-prone skin), focus on balancing and refining pores; for Paula’s Choice (ideal for dry/sensitive skin), allow the nourishing formula to soothe and replenish; and for Fresh Rose Toner (great for dehydration), let the hydrating rosewater prep skin for serums

Ingredient Spotlight

Science-backed actives for specific concerns

Hyaluronic acid

Benefits: Intense hydration, plumps skin, reduces fine lines

Best for: Dryness, dehydration, aging concerns

Key Facts:

  • Holds 1000x its weight in water
  • Works for all skin types
  • Use morning and night

How To Use Hyaluronic Acid Water Sun Gel:

To use the Hyaluronic Acid Water Sun Gel, start with cleansed and toned skin, then apply a generous, even layer (about a nickel-sized amount) to your face, neck, and any exposed areas. Gently pat or smooth it in, avoiding harsh rubbing, and let it absorb for 1–2 minutes before applying makeup (if needed). Since it contains hyaluronic acid, applying it to slightly damp skin can boost hydration, but always ensure full absorption to prevent pilling

Avoid These Skincare Mistakes

❌

Over-Exfoliating

Using harsh scrubs or acids (AHA/BHA) too often can strip your skin barrier, leading to irritation, redness, and breakouts. Limit exfoliation to 2–3 times per week (less for sensitive skin).

❌

Using Hot Water

Hot showers or face-washing strips natural oils, leading to dryness. Stick to lukewarm water

Dermatologist Tips

Professional advice for optimal skin health

"Layer Products Correctly"

Dr. Sarah Chen, Board-Certified Dermatologist

"Always apply products from thinnest to thickest consistency. Start with water-based serums, then oils, and finish with creams."
